Magical Bridge Claudelands Park


Why Claudelands Park


The Magical Bridge Trust commissioned Brodiehailwood to produce an Inclusive Playground Concept to transform the old, tired, existing destination playground located at Claudelands Park. The Claudelands Park playground was due for renewal by the Hamilton City Council. A partnership was formed with the Council whereby the budget allocated for the old playground's renewal was made available to the trust, which fundraised a further $2m for the phase 1 budget, to build New Zealand's first Magical Bridge Playground. Phase 1 is now open and is being enthusiastically received by the entire community. Funding for the last few items in Phase 1 is underway with a Givealittle page now receiving donations. Phase 2 funding is underway to add some key items such as a toilet block, musical lazer harp, accessible fitness zone, sensory dome, and artwork.
The Claudelands Park location was chosen from a range of options around Hamilton City but was identified as ideal due to its existing facilities including restrooms, accessible car parks, picnic areas, shaded seating, and mature trees. Claudelands Park is easily accessible by public transport and is located within a diverse community - surrounded by young and elderly residents. The existing mounding and zip lines in the park have been
retained for the new playground.
